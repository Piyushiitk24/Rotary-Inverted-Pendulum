\chapter{Full-Surface Sliding Mode Controller (Four-State Surface)}
\label{ch:smc4}

\section{Base tracking errors}
The base terms in SMC4 are written in tracking-error form so the same controller can cover both hold-at-center ($\theta_{\mathrm{ref}}=\mathrm{const}$) and commanded moves (nudge mode, time-varying $\theta_{\mathrm{ref}}$). Define
\[
\theta_{\mathrm{err}} \equiv \mathrm{diff}(\theta,\theta_{\mathrm{ref}}),\qquad
\dot{\theta}_{\mathrm{err}} \equiv \dot{\theta}-\dot{\theta}_{\mathrm{ref}},
\]
with the wrap-safe operator $\mathrm{diff}(\cdot,\cdot)$ from Chapter~\ref{ch:notation}. The reference acceleration $\ddot{\theta}_{\mathrm{ref}}$ appears as a feedforward term in the surface dynamics.

\section{Full-state sliding surface}
SMC4 extends the pendulum-only sliding surface by adding base-tracking error terms:
\begin{equation}
s \equiv \dot{\alpha} + \lambda_{\alpha}\alpha
  + k\Bigl(\dot{\theta}_{\mathrm{err}} + \lambda_{\theta}\theta_{\mathrm{err}}\Bigr),
\label{eq:smc4_surface}
\end{equation}
with design parameters $\lambda_{\alpha}>0$, $\lambda_{\theta}>0$, and coupling weight $k\ge 0$. The combined base term
\[
\dot{\theta}_{\mathrm{err}} + \lambda_{\theta}\theta_{\mathrm{err}}
\]
is just a single signal that says how far the base is from the desired tracking behavior. If $k=0$, \eqref{eq:smc4_surface} reduces to the pendulum-only surface used in Chapter~\ref{ch:smc}.

\section{Surface dynamics and control effectiveness}
Differentiating \eqref{eq:smc4_surface} gives
\begin{equation}
\dot{s} = \ddot{\alpha} + \lambda_{\alpha}\dot{\alpha}
        + k\ddot{\theta}_{\mathrm{err}} + k\lambda_{\theta}\dot{\theta}_{\mathrm{err}}.
\label{eq:smc4_sdot_start}
\end{equation}
Using $\ddot{\theta}_{\mathrm{err}}=\ddot{\theta}-\ddot{\theta}_{\mathrm{ref}}$ and substituting the nonlinear relation \eqref{eq:smc_nonlinear_relation} yields
\begin{equation}
\dot{s}
=
\underbrace{\Bigl(A\sin\alpha+\sin\alpha\cos\alpha\,\dot{\theta}^2\Bigr)}_{\triangleq f_0(\alpha,\dot{\theta})}
\;+\;
\lambda_{\alpha}\dot{\alpha}
\;+\;
k\lambda_{\theta}\dot{\theta}_{\mathrm{err}}
\;-\;
k\ddot{\theta}_{\mathrm{ref}}
\;+\;
(k-B\cos\alpha)\ddot{\theta}.
\label{eq:smc4_sdot}
\end{equation}
The important point is the factor $(B\cos\alpha-k)$. If $k$ gets too close to $B\cos\alpha$, the controller loses authority and asks for very large accelerations. That is why the firmware protects the denominator.

\section{Reaching law and complete control law}
As in Chapter~\ref{ch:smc}, use the boundary-layer reaching law
\[
\dot{s} = -K\,\mathrm{sat}\!\left(\frac{s}{\phi}\right),\qquad K>0,\ \phi>0.
\]
Substituting into \eqref{eq:smc4_sdot} gives the analytic control law
\begin{equation}
\boxed{
\ddot{\theta}
  =
  \frac{
    f_0(\alpha,\dot{\theta})
    + \lambda_{\alpha}\dot{\alpha}
    + k\lambda_{\theta}\dot{\theta}_{\mathrm{err}}
    - k\ddot{\theta}_{\mathrm{ref}}
    + K\,\mathrm{sat}\!\left(\dfrac{s}{\phi}\right)
  }{
    B\cos\alpha - k
  }
}
\label{eq:smc4_control_law}
\end{equation}
with $s$ given by \eqref{eq:smc4_surface}. Equation~\eqref{eq:smc4_control_law} is the \emph{analytic form}. The firmware does not use $k$ directly at runtime; it uses an effective coupling $k_{\mathrm{Eff}}$ after ramping and denominator protection.

\section{Lyapunov stability analysis (ideal reaching law)}
As in the hybrid controller, the reaching law is designed to drive the sliding variable $s$ in \eqref{eq:smc4_surface} toward zero. Choose the Lyapunov candidate
\[
V(s)=\frac{1}{2}s^2,\qquad V\ge 0.
\]
With the boundary-layer reaching law $\dot{s}=-K\,\mathrm{sat}(s/\phi)$ \cite{utkin1992,slotine1991,Young1999},
\[
\dot{V}=s\dot{s}=-K\,s\,\mathrm{sat}\!\left(\frac{s}{\phi}\right)\le 0.
\]
Therefore $V$ never increases and the ideal analytic law drives $s$ toward the sliding surface.

\paragraph{Theory versus firmware.}
As in Chapter~\ref{ch:smc}, this Lyapunov argument applies to the analytic law before practical limits are added. The real firmware also includes $k$ ramping, denominator-margin protection, clamped $\theta_{\mathrm{err}}$, clamped $\dot{\theta}_{\mathrm{err}}$, clamped internal $\dot{\alpha}$, extra acceleration and speed caps, sign handling, and a gated base assist. These protections are necessary for the stepper-driven platform, but they mean the ideal reaching result is not a full hardware guarantee.

\section{Implementation safeguards and stepper-friendly shaping}
\label{sec:smc4_safeguards}
Compared to hybrid SMC, SMC4 is more sensitive because the coupling term appears both in the surface and in the denominator. The firmware therefore adds several safeguards:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Upright-only and cosine floor:} enforce $|\alpha|<\SI{25}{\degree}$ and use a protected $\cos_{\mathrm{safe}}=\mathrm{sgn}(\cos\alpha)\max(|\cos\alpha|,0.2)$.
  \item \textbf{Limit coupling:} enforce $k\le k_{\max}=1.20$.
  \item \textbf{Denominator margin:} enforce $|B\cos_{\mathrm{safe}}-k_{\mathrm{Eff}}|\ge \mathrm{den}_{\min}=0.60$.
  \item \textbf{Smooth engage:} ramp the coupling from zero to its requested value over \SI{200}{ms}. The diagnostic fields \texttt{kRamp}, \texttt{kEff}, and \texttt{den} printed by the firmware show this protection in action.
  \item \textbf{Practical clamps:} clamp $|\theta_{\mathrm{err}}|\le \SI{78}{\degree}$, clamp $|\dot{\theta}_{\mathrm{err}}|\le \SI{200}{\degree\per\second}$, clamp the internal $\dot{\alpha}$ used in the computation to $|\dot{\alpha}|\le \SI{200}{\degree\per\second}$, and apply tighter caps $u_{\max,\mathrm{SMC4}}=\SI{12000}{\step\per\second\squared}$ and $\dot{\theta}_{\max,\mathrm{SMC4}}=\SI{2000}{\step\per\second}$.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Gated base-centering assist (implemented)}
Although the surface \eqref{eq:smc4_surface} already includes base error terms, the firmware also adds a small gated base-centering assist to reduce slow drift once the pendulum is already near upright. This assist uses the same practical centering idea introduced in Chapter~\ref{ch:smc}. It is scaled by $\gamma$ (default $\gamma=1.0$) and enabled only after a \SI{200}{ms} grace period and when $|\alpha|<\SI{5}{\degree}$ and $|\dot{\alpha}|<\SI{250}{\degree\per\second}$.
